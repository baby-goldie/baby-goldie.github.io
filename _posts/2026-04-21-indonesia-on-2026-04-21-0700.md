1.1 Indonesia announced a major natural gas discovery in the Geliga Block, offshore East Kalimantan, by Italy's Eni SpA. Preliminary estimates suggest 5 trillion cubic feet (Tcf) of gas and 300 million barrels of condensate, a find expected to significantly bolster national energy security and production targets for 2028 and 2030. This discovery, which includes the participation of China Petroleum & Chemical Corp (ticker: 600028, PetroChina, 中国石油), highlights the Kutai Basin's untapped hydrocarbon potential and Eni's ongoing exploration success in the region.

3.1 The July 1, 2026, deadline for the full implementation of the B50 biodiesel program represents a major risk factor for Indonesia's fiscal balance and supply chain stability. The success of this transition depends on the availability of sufficient CPO feedstock and the potential impact of reduced CPO exports on the national trade balance. Monitoring the price gap between crude oil and crude palm oil will be crucial for assessing the economic viability and potential subsidy burden of this ambitious energy mandate.
