## 1. Key Events

1.1 The Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS) has implemented a slight tightening of its monetary policy for the first time since 2022. This action involves increasing the rate of appreciation of the Singapore dollar nominal effective exchange rate (S$NEER) policy band, a direct response to rising global energy prices and supply chain constraints stemming from the ongoing conflict in the Middle East. Consequently, MAS has raised its headline and core inflation forecasts for 2026 to between 1.5% and 2.5%, emphasizing the need to mitigate imported inflation in an uncertain global economic environment.
